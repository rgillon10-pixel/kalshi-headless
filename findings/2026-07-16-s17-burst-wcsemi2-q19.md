## 4. Lead-lag robustness — the poly-lead is a ONE-TICK artifact (L57)

Two leave-one-out cuts, both reported honestly:

**(a) Drop the biggest RAW combined move (the naive "drop the largest-move capture"):** on this tape
the largest raw move is a **contemporaneous collapse** (ENG dK −0.38 / dP −0.40 at the 20:53 match
resolution), where BOTH venues moved together in one capture and neither led — so dropping it does
NOT test the lead and, if anything, *strengthens* the poly-leads ρ (it removes lagged noise). This
is a trap: at burst resolution the biggest price move is not necessarily the lead driver.

**(b) Drop the single lag-pair that DRIVES the poly-leads correlation (the honest L57 test):** the
probe identifies and removes the one (Δpoly@t, Δkalshi@t+1) pair with the largest cross-product
contribution — here the **20:15→20:17 first-goal pair** (Polymarket moved 0.22 at 20:15, Kalshi
caught up 0.25 at 20:17):

| ticker | ρ(poly leads) FULL | drop the single driving lag-pair |
|---|---|---|
| KXWCROUND-26FINAL-ENG | 0.269 | **0.054** |
| KXWCROUND-26FINAL-ARG | 0.290 | **0.053** |

The "Polymarket leads Kalshi" magnitude is driven **almost entirely by the single 20:15 first-goal
lag-pair**. Removing it collapses both tickers' poly-leads ρ from ~0.27–0.29 to ~0.05 — statistically
indistinguishable from their near-zero kalshi-leads ρ. **Reported honestly: the burst lead-lag
figure is NOT a robust persistent lead — it is the first-goal snapshot (Polymarket's book repriced
one 2-min capture before Kalshi's) expressed as a Pearson ρ over ~25–29 near-flat steps plus one
large shock step.** It corroborates the §3 shock-snapshot story but is not an independent statistical
lead-lag relationship. This is exactly the CPI leg's finding (L57), reproduced on the WC schema.

## 5. FILLABILITY CAVEATS (mandatory — these keep the claim honest)

1. **SIZE-BLIND.** `tape/polymarket_pairs/` records carry **no size/depth fields** — only
   `best_ask` / `best_bid` on the Poly side and top-of-book asks/bids on the Kalshi side. Every
   dislocation above is scored on top-of-book price alone and **cannot be claimed fillable at any
   volume**. A 12–18¢ gap on a book with $1 of resting size is not a tradeable edge.
2. **NON-SYNCHRONOUS QUOTE RISK at the shock instant.** `captured_at` is a per-record timestamp but
   the two tickers' records in one pass are near-simultaneous within a capture; the tape cannot
   establish cross-venue quote simultaneity at sub-capture resolution. The single-capture 20:15
   dislocations are exactly the shape a **stale-Kalshi-quote repricing-lag artifact** takes (Kalshi
   yes_ask 0.52/0.49 unchanged from 20:13, collapsing to 0.77/0.24 by the next pass) — **gone by the
   time you could cross both venues**. A transient that lives one 2-min capture is not crossable.
3. **CROSS-VENUE SETTLEMENT + CAPITAL-RAIL RISK.** S9/S17's premise is that USD/KYC vs USDC/Polygon
   rail segmentation is what *prevents* arbitrage from enforcing parity. A "locked pair" therefore
   carries cross-venue settlement + capital-rail risk and is **NOT a realised P&L** — it is an
   outcome-neutral position on paper, exposed to the very segmentation that is the thesis.

## Lesson candidates (for the kb-distiller)

- **The naive "drop the largest raw move" LOO can MISFIRE at burst resolution — drop the largest
  cross-product LAG-PAIR instead.** On this WC tape the biggest raw price move (the 20:53
  match-resolution collapse) was a *contemporaneous* move that never contributed to the poly-lead;
  dropping it *strengthened* the lead ρ. The honest L57 test drops the single (Δpoly@t, Δkalshi@t+1)
  pair with the largest contribution to that direction's ρ — which cleanly collapsed poly-leads
  0.27/0.29 → 0.05. Sharpens L57: for a directional lead claim, leave-one-out on the correlation's
  own driver pair, not on the biggest price change. (Family L57 / L8 lagged confound / L13 startup
  artifact — a single dominant observation masquerading as a population statistic.)
- **The WIDTH × DURATION discriminator generalizes cleanly from the CPI (Fed) schema to the WC-round
  schema.** Same structural family (`polymarket_pairs.v1`), same result: the shock dislocation is
  large + single-capture; nothing large is durable. Reinforces L57's cross-venue generalization of
  L31 — a real information-shock gap is short-lived, a persistent gap is stale/segmented nominal.
- **The Q31 Poly-fee correction is load-bearing for cross-venue burst scans and must be a rate, not
  a flat constant.** At Poly rate 0.0 (the stale s17 assumption) the WC scan manufactured two
  marginal "edges" (net −$0.0024 / −$0.0125 at the real 0.05 rate) that are float-dust zeros
  fee-free. Any cross-venue dislocation scan must charge BOTH legs' real fees from `core.pricing`
  (`fee_per_contract` + `polymarket_fee_per_contract`) and report the fee-free view only as a tagged
  sensitivity. (Family L5/L18 fee discipline, now extended to the Polymarket leg.)
- **`tape/polymarket_pairs/` (WC-round) is size-blind**, exactly like `tape/polymarket_macro_pairs/`
  (Fed) — only best_ask/best_bid, no depth. Every cross-venue dislocation over it is a price
  observation, never a fillability claim. A future S9/S17 fill-scale test needs Polymarket L2 depth,
  which this tape family does not carry. (Family L39/L48/L57 — a proxy orients but never substitutes
  for a size/queue-aware fill test.)
