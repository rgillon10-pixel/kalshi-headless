**`real_ask` caveat (stated, not hidden):** `yes_ask.close_dollars` is the *published top-of-book

`real_ask` is the correct tag for "what was the published taker ask." But it is **BBO-at-candle-

between candles. The tag is right for what it is; it overstates *fillable size*. Degenerate
post-settlement candles (`yes_ask==1.0000` with `yes_bid==0.0000` → no NO liquidity to lift)

## Method

### Kalshi side (Hard Rule #3 throughout)
Per period: `bracket_sum` = Σ of the 5 legs' `yes_ask.close`; Kalshi P(hold) =
`core.pricing.normalized_ask(yes_ask_H0, bracket_sum)`; `overround_absorbed =
core.pricing.overround(asks)`. A period is **usable only if all 5 legs are priced** (complete
partition) — 214/352 hourly periods complete; of those 163 joined a ZQ daily close. Persisted
per period: `raw_yes_ask`, `bracket_sum`, `overround_absorbed`, `member_count=5`,
`price_source_tag="real_ask"`.

### ZQ side — the CME-FedWatch month-average identity (formula)
A 30-day Fed Funds future settles to `100 − (average daily EFFR over the contract month)`.
Split June (30d) at the decision **effective** date (FOMC decides 2026-06-17, effective the next
day → June 18): `N_pre = 17` (Jun 1–17, old rate), `N_post = 13` (Jun 18–30, new rate).

```
implied_avg_rate = 100 − ZQ_close                         # avg daily EFFR over June
r_post = (implied_avg_rate · 30 − N_pre · r_pre) / N_post  # solve for expected post-meeting rate
```

Map `r_post` onto the nearest 25bp grid `{cut25=3.375, hold=3.625, hike25=3.875}` by two-node
interpolation → weight on HOLD = `zq_p_hold`. Tag `synthetic` (a derived probability is NOT a
fill — it is correctly the *least*-trusted tag).

**Assumptions (every one stated):** A1 EFFR == target-range midpoint; A2 current range
3.50–3.75% (Fed's last move 2025-12-11, midpoint 3.625% — confirmed externally *and* internally:
ZQ June implied avg ≈ 3.62% ≈ the midpoint, i.e. the market priced ≈ no change all month); A3
June = 30 days, decision effective June 18 → 17/13 split; A4 only the 25bp grid carries weight
(the >25bp legs priced ~1¢ each — confirmed in the candles).

### EFFR look-ahead leak screen (peer-review flag — how we avoided it)
A naive backout uses the *realized* June EFFR average to infer `r_post` — but that average
includes post-meeting days whose EFFR **is the answer**, leaking the outcome into a pre-meeting
probability. We avoid it: **(i)** `r_pre` is the *current* target midpoint (3.625%), a constant
known on every date `t`, never a realized future daily-EFFR average; **(ii)** `implied_avg_rate`
comes from the **ZQ price on date `t`** (a forward-looking market quote), never realized
post-meeting EFFR; **(iii)** the only "post" quantity, `r_post`, is *solved from the on-`t` ZQ
price*. Every input to `P(hold)[t]` is known on `t`. No realized post-2026-06-17 EFFR print is
ever read.

### Basis + fee model
`basis[t] = Kalshi P(hold)[t] − ZQ P(hold)[t]`. **Not a hedge** — Kalshi halts 1:59pm / settles
~2:05pm, so the Kalshi leg is a *frozen directional pre-position* with unbounded per-event
downside (dossier caveat). Marked to settlement (H0 = YES → $1.00). Net of:
- **Kalshi taker fee** = `ceil(0.07 · p·(1−p) · contracts)` cents (`config/venues.yaml`
  `fee_coeff=0.07`); at p≈0.95 this is ≈$0.0033/contract → 1¢ after the cents-round-up.
- **ZQ commission** = $0.012/contract (conservative retail round-turn).
- **ZQ slippage** = 1 ZQ tick, charged as a ~$0.005 implied-prob haircut.

## VERDICT (honest)

- **(a) n = 1.** No statistical claim, no bootstrapped CI is possible. Full stop. Nothing here
  graduates anything.
- **(b) June was LOW-INFORMATION.** P(hold) ∈ [0.93, 0.98] on both venues throughout — a
  near-certain hold that was never contested. The basis on it is **uninformative about a
  contested-meeting edge**: you cannot learn the size of a disagreement from a meeting where
  both sides agreed.
- **(c) Structure: clean.** ~3.4¢ overround vs ~10¢ weather — prob-to-prob comparison is real
  here. The thesis that FOMC brackets dodge the overround killer **holds on this event**.
- **(d) Net-of-fee: no edge on this event** (mean −1.39¢; only 5/163 periods even net positive,
  max +0.82¢). But (a)/(b): one near-certain-hold event proves nothing either way.

### A material caveat surfaced by the data: ZQ-prob sensitivity
ZQ P(hold) swung 0.931→0.977 off a **1-tick** ZQ price move (96.375→96.385). The month-average
identity divides by `N_post = 13`, so it **amplifies** tiny ZQ moves into large implied-prob
swings. Daily ZQ close granularity is therefore *too coarse* to read a clean P(hold) — a single
tick of rounding noise dominates the basis. This is independent evidence that **intraday ZQ ticks
are required**, not optional, for any real test.

### Prior-meeting illustrative pull — deferred (honest note)
The spec allowed pulling one contested prior meeting (the 2025 cutting cycle had real
disagreement). The 2025 KXFEDDECISION events exist (`25SEP`, `25OCT`, `25JUL`, …) but their legs
use a **different, target-range ticker scheme** (not the `H0/H25/H26/C25/C26` move-delta scheme
this script parses), and discovery hit hard rate limits (HTTP 429). Rather than fake a parse, the
illustrative pull is **deferred to the gated CME version**, which will handle both schemes. June
alone delivers the structure check; its low-information nature is itself the key honest finding.

## What the Ryan-gated CME version adds (spec)

The first cut answered "is the structure clean?" (yes) and "is the free data enough?" (no). A
real test — the thing that could produce an actual edge claim — needs:

1. **Intraday ZQ ticks** (Databento free $125 credit, or CME via a broker feed). Daily close is
   too coarse: 1 tick of ZQ noise swings the implied P(hold) by ~4¢ through the `N_post` divisor.
   Intraday lets the basis be read at the same minute on both venues.
2. **Many meetings, weighted to CONTESTED ones.** ~8 FOMC/year; the edge (if any) lives in
   meetings the market genuinely disputed (cut-vs-hold odds near 50/50), not near-certain holds.
   Pull every meeting back through the 2025 cutting cycle (handling the target-range ticker
   scheme), tag each by contestedness, and study the basis only where it can be non-trivial.
3. **A real CI.** With many contested-meeting observations, block-bootstrap the per-event net P&L
   (block = meeting) and require the lower bound to **strictly clear zero at real asks** before
   one dollar moves (prime directive). Until then S2 stays `binding-test-defined`.
4. **Kalshi L2 depth at the same instants** (not just BBO-at-candle-close), so the `real_ask` is
   a *fillable-size* ask, not just top-of-book. The first cut's `real_ask` tag is honest about
   being BBO-only; the real test must price the walk-the-book cost.
5. **The frozen-pre-position risk, modeled explicitly.** Kalshi halts before settlement, so this
   is a directional bet with unbounded per-event downside, not a basis lock — size it as a
   micro-position, never as a "hedge" (dossier dead-list: the K1' hedge category error).

**Recommendation:** the structure is clean enough to justify the modest CME data spend for a
proper multi-meeting, intraday, contested-weighted test **— gated on Ryan**. Do **not** treat
this n=1 near-certain-hold result as evidence of an edge; treat it as a green light to *fund the
data*, nothing more.
