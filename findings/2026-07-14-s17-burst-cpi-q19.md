## 3. Fillable cross-venue dislocations — WIDTH × DURATION discrimination

25 captures cleared BOTH fees (`net_edge > 0`) across 11 episodes. Fee model: **Kalshi taker fee
on both crossing legs** via `core.pricing.fee_per_contract` (crossing to buy the ask OR sell the
bid both lift resting size — neither is a free maker fill, the S13/L5 lesson); **Polymarket fee =
0.0**, a tagged model assumption (`assumed_zero_polymarket_clob`), NOT a fill.

The observed dislocations split **cleanly** along the S6/L31 stale-quote-artifact discriminator
the milestone requires — width and duration are anti-correlated, exactly the wrong shape for a
live arb:

**(a) LARGE + SHORT — shock-repricing transients (single-capture, 0s):**

| pair | direction | max net_edge | duration | captures | timestamp |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| KXFEDDECISION-26JUL-H25 | buy_poly_sell_kalshi | **$0.0790** | 0s | 1 | 12:30:13.19Z |
| KXFEDDECISION-26JUL-H0  | buy_kalshi_sell_poly | **$0.0600** | 0s | 1 | 12:30:13.19Z |

The two largest dislocations land at **EXACTLY 12:30:13Z — the CPI release instant** — on the two
July buckets, and each exists in **a single 60s capture only** (`duration = 0s`, gone by the next
capture). The raw quotes show the mechanism: at 12:30:13 the 26JUL-H25 Polymarket ask had already
repriced 0.299→**0.181** (toward "hold") while Kalshi still sat at yes_ask **0.29** (unchanged from
12:29); by 12:31:10 Kalshi caught up to 0.16. This is a **shock-repricing transient**, not a
resting arb.

**(b) SMALL + PERSISTENT — stale / segmentation nominal-basis (S6/L31 signature):**

| pair | direction | max net_edge | mean | duration | captures | window |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| KXFEDDECISION-26SEP-H0  | buy_kalshi_sell_poly | $0.0400 | $0.0150 | **297s** | 6 | 12:32→12:37 |
| KXFEDDECISION-26SEP-H0  | buy_kalshi_sell_poly | $0.0100 | $0.0033 | **302s** | 6 | 13:40→13:45 |
| KXFEDDECISION-26SEP-H25 | buy_poly_sell_kalshi | $0.0200 | $0.0167 | 116s | 3 | 12:32→12:34 |

The **durable** dislocations are all **small (≤$0.04, mostly $0.01)** and sit on the **September**
(less CPI-sensitive) buckets, persisting many minutes across 3–6 captures — the classic
signature of a **stale / thin nominal quote nobody is resting real size behind** (L31), the same
artifact family that produced S6's spurious +$0.069 "edge." A $0.01–0.04 gap that survives
5 minutes across 6 consecutive captures is not a repeatedly-fillable arb.

**Conclusion (the discriminator's verdict): NO dislocation is both LARGE and DURABLE.** The large
ones ($0.06–0.08) are single-capture release-instant transients; the persistent ones ($0.01–0.04)
carry the S6/L31 stale/segmentation signature. **There is no clean fillable shock-scale
cross-venue edge in this window.**

## 4. Lead-lag robustness — the July signal is a ONE-TICK artifact

Recomputing the July buckets' `ρ(poly leads)` with the release-instant capture removed
(dropping captures within ±90s of 12:30:13, and separately dropping just the single 12:30:13 tick):

| pair | ρ(poly leads) FULL | drop ±90s | drop only the 12:30:13 tick |
|---|---|---|---|
| KXFEDDECISION-26JUL-H25 | 0.902 | **0.196** | 0.149 |
| KXFEDDECISION-26JUL-H0  | 0.777 | **0.037** | 0.043 |

The "Polymarket leads Kalshi" magnitude is driven **almost entirely by the single 12:30:13
release tick**. Removing it collapses 26JUL-H25's poly-leads ρ from 0.90 to ~0.15–0.20 (now
statistically indistinguishable from its kalshi-leads ρ of ~0.19) and 26JUL-H0's from 0.78 to
~0.04 (gone entirely). **Reported honestly: the correlation-based lead-lag figure is NOT a robust
persistent lead — it is the release-instant snapshot itself, expressed as a Pearson ρ.** It
corroborates the shock-snapshot story (§3a: Poly's book moved first at 12:30:13) but does not
constitute a robust statistical lead-lag relationship independent of that one tick.

## 5. FILLABILITY CAVEATS (mandatory — these keep the claim honest)

1. **SIZE-BLIND.** `tape/polymarket_macro_pairs/` records carry **no size/depth fields** — only
   `best_ask` / `best_bid` (confirmed against the record schema). Every dislocation above is scored
   on top-of-book price alone and **cannot be claimed fillable at any volume**. A 6–8¢ gap on a
   book with $1 of resting size is not a tradeable edge.
2. **NON-SYNCHRONOUS QUOTE RISK at the release instant.** The single-capture 12:30:13 dislocations
   are measured across a within-pass **sequential** read of the two venues (captures are not
   perfectly synchronous — min gap 3.52s). At the release instant a 6–10¢ gap that exists for
   <60s is exactly the shape a **non-synchronous-quote artifact** takes, and it is **gone by the
   time you could cross both venues**. A transient that lives one 60s capture is not crossable.
3. **CROSS-VENUE SETTLEMENT + CAPITAL-RAIL RISK.** S17's own premise is that USD/KYC vs
   USDC/Polygon rail segmentation is what *prevents* arbitrage from enforcing parity. A "locked
   pair" therefore carries cross-venue settlement + capital-rail risk and is **NOT a realised
   P&L** — it is an outcome-neutral position on paper, exposed to the very segmentation that is
   S17's thesis.

## Independent verification (two-agent rule)

An independent `verifier` re-derived every descriptive number from the raw tape and
adversarially attacked both tradeable claims. Result: **descriptive numbers CONFIRMED;
both tradeable-edge claims REFUTED** — no claim is safe to record as CONFIRMED, all stay
PROVISIONAL, and S17's registry does not flip (decision deferred to FOMC Jul 29).

- **Dislocation arithmetic — CONFIRMED.** The 25 captures / 11 episodes reproduce; the two
  headline edges recompute exactly at the **taker** rate (`core.pricing.fee_per_contract`,
  fee $0.02 on each): 26JUL-H25 `0.28 − 0.181 − 0.02 = +0.0790`, 26JUL-H0
  `0.79 − 0.71 − 0.02 = +0.0600`. Fee rate correct (taker 0.07, not hand-rolled — L5/L18).
- **Dislocation fillability — REFUTED.** The schema is **size-blind** (no depth field). More
  decisively, `captured_at` is a **single pass-level timestamp shared by all 15 records in a
  capture**, so it cannot establish venue simultaneity — the 12:30:13 gap is a **stale-Kalshi-
  quote repricing-lag artifact** (Kalshi `yes_ask` 0.29 unchanged from 12:29, collapsing to
  0.16 by the next capture), not a crossable arb. The durable small episodes carry the
  S6/L31 stale-nominal-quote signature.
- **Lead-lag — REFUTED as a directional claim.** Independently, removing the release tick not
  only collapses ρ(poly leads) but the **residual sign is unstable** — on the verifier's
  leave-one-out it flips toward *Kalshi* (26JUL-H25 ρ_poly 0.90→~0.33 while ρ_kalshi rises to
  ~0.39; 26JUL-H0 0.78→~0.12, ρ_kalshi ~0.41). So **no sign-stable directional lead-lag claim
  is defensible.** The only defensible statement is the factual 12:30:13 snapshot description
  (Kalshi's quote was momentarily stale while Polymarket's had repriced) — a single n=1 event,
  not a measured lead.
- **Hard Rule #3 — CONFIRMED clean.** The probe reads only `polymarket_macro_pairs/`
  (both legs `real_ask`) and excludes the `synthetic` CPI leg — no synthetic value leaks into
  the real-ask comparison.

**Bottom line (verifier):** the burst tape is adequate and the descriptive picture is solid,
but there is **no CONFIRMED tradeable edge** — the directional lead-lag is a single-tick,
sign-unstable artifact and the dislocations are size-blind, non-synchronous, or small
stale-nominal-basis. Everything remains PROVISIONAL; S17 stays `data-collecting`.

## Lesson candidates (for the kb-distiller)

- **A burst-resolution lead-lag ρ can be a single-tick artifact.** On the July buckets, ρ(poly
  leads) of 0.90 / 0.78 collapsed to 0.15–0.20 / 0.04 once the single release-instant capture was
  removed — the "lead" was that one snapshot expressed as a correlation, not a persistent
  relationship. Any burst-window lead-lag figure should be reported *with* the release-tick-removed
  recompute; the headline ρ over ~100 near-flat steps plus one large shock step is dominated by the
  shock step. (Family: L8 lagged-confound / L13 startup-artifact — a single dominant observation
  masquerading as a population statistic.)
- **The WIDTH × DURATION cross is a clean cross-venue-arb discriminator, and it separated perfectly
  here.** Large edges were single-capture transients; durable edges were small S6/L31 nominal-basis
  gaps — no overlap. This is the burst-tape realization of the prep milestone's hypothesis and
  generalizes L31 from single-venue wings to cross-venue dislocations: a real information-shock gap
  is short-lived; a persistent gap is a stale/segmented nominal quote.
- **`tape/polymarket_macro_pairs/` is size-blind** (only `best_ask`/`best_bid`, no depth) — every
  cross-venue dislocation scored over it is a price observation, never a fillability claim. A future
  S17 fill-scale test needs Polymarket L2 depth, which this tape family does not carry. (Family:
  L39/L48 — a generous proxy orients but never substitutes for a queue/size-aware fill test.)
